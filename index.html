DOC-002 // ABOUT

From the Shop Floor to the Dashboard

I spent the first fifteen-plus years of my career on the production floor of automotive manufacturing plants — troubleshooting robotic welders, keeping CNC lines in spec, and chasing down the root cause of every unplanned stop before it turned into a missed shift target. I've worked inside PLC cabinets, rebuilt hydraulic presses at 2 a.m., and sat in more downtime review meetings than I can count.

Somewhere along the way I realized the most valuable thing I was producing wasn't just uptime — it was the pattern behind it. Every work order, every MTTR log, every changeover sheet was a dataset waiting to be read. That realization is what pulled me into data analytics: Excel modeling, SQL querying, Power BI dashboards, and Python-driven analysis, all pointed at the same floor I know inside and out.

Today I build the kind of tools I wish I'd had as a technician — dashboards that tell a maintenance team exactly where to look, and reports that give plant managers a straight answer on where the next dollar of reliability investment should go.
